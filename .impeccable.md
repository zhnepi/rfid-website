## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are international B2B buyers and system integrators in transportation, hospitality, healthcare, event management, and access control. They visit the site to evaluate factory-direct RFID hardware at volume, confirm product fit, and decide whether to send an inquiry for samples, pricing, customization, or compatibility support.

### Brand Personality
Professional, experienced, factory.

The interface should feel credible, capable, and commercially mature. It should communicate that ProudTek is a long-running manufacturing partner that understands industrial requirements, customization, and export-ready procurement. It should not feel cheap, flimsy, gimmicky, or like a generic trading template.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a mobile-first B2B manufacturer aesthetic: structured, confident, and operationally clear. Keep the site visually polished, but favor commercial trust, technical competence, and factory experience over luxury theatrics or flashy startup styling. Prioritize fast comprehension, proof, product fit, and clear inquiry paths for international buyers scanning on mobile.

### Design Principles
1. Make inquiry paths obvious. Every major page should help buyers move toward a quote, sample request, or compatibility discussion without friction.
2. Lead with competence and proof. Emphasize manufacturing experience, customization ability, export readiness, and application fit before decorative styling.
3. Optimize for mobile buying behavior. Content should scan cleanly on small screens, with touch-friendly actions, compressed copy, and reduced friction in key decision moments.
4. Use clear commercial language. Replace vague marketing phrasing with direct, buyer-friendly wording that helps international B2B visitors understand what to do next.
5. Avoid anything that feels cheap. Favor restrained, durable visual choices and consistent systems over clutter, gimmicks, or overly promotional UI.
