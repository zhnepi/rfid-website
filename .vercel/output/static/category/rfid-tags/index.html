Buying Guide

RFID Tags buying guide

RFID tag selection depends on surface material, environment, required read range and how the tag will be mounted. A good tag match improves read consistency much more than simply choosing the lowest-price inlay.

How to choose the right rfid tags

  1. 1

    Start with the mounting surface. Metal, liquid and textile environments need different tag constructions.

  2. 2

    Decide whether the tag must survive heat, pressure, detergents, UV exposure or repeated bending.

  3. 3

    Confirm required read distance in the real environment rather than lab conditions.

  4. 4

    For washable or reusable assets, ask how many cycles the tag should survive before replacement.

  5. 5

    When two tag options seem close, test both on-site with your actual reader and process flow.

FAQ

Questions buyers often ask

How do I choose between a standard tag and an anti-metal tag? +

If the tag will be installed directly on metal or close to a metal surface, use an anti-metal design. Standard tags usually lose range and stability on metal.

Can laundry tags survive industrial washing and drying? +

Yes, but the exact tag model matters. Share your wash temperature, pressure, chemical exposure and attachment method so we can recommend the correct laundry tag.

What is the most important test before a bulk RFID tag order? +

An on-site sample test with your real reader, antenna placement and tagged object is the best way to confirm read reliability before production.

Can ProudTek supply tags pre-encoded or serialized? +

Yes. We can provide blank tags, sequential numbering, UID print or custom data writing depending on the chip and your process.

Can RFID laundry tags survive autoclave sterilization? +

Silicone-encapsulated UHF laundry tags can withstand temperatures up to 200°C and are suitable for autoclave cycles. Textile laundry tags handle up to 85°C and 200+ industrial wash cycles. Share your sterilization protocol for a specific recommendation.

What is the read range for anti-metal RFID tags? +

Anti-metal UHF RFID tags typically achieve 1-8 meters read range depending on tag size, chip sensitivity, and reader power. Smaller on-metal tags (30x15mm) read at 1-3 meters, while larger pallet-size tags can reach 8+ meters.

Which RFID tag works best for outdoor asset tracking? +

For outdoor use, ABS-encapsulated or ceramic RFID tags with IP67 or IP68 ratings are recommended. These withstand UV exposure, rain, dust, and temperature extremes from -40°C to 85°C. UHF frequency is preferred for long-range bulk scanning.
